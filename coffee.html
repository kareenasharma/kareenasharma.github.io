Coffee

A mobile application for patrons of the Chicago Public Library. Through Coffee, patrons receive personalized recommendations of media and activities offered by the library to engage in. Additionally, they can connect with their community through their friends' activity feed and by sharing and reading reviews.

Year: 2019

Teammates: Alyssa Zhong, Caryl Henry, Julia Brain

Background

With the rapid developments in technology, libraries have seen a significant decrease in community engagement. We wanted to determine how we could redefine libraries for the 21st century. Specifically, we wanted to know how we might foster a stronger community by increasing the engagement of library patrons. We conducted observations at libraries, studied articles online, and interviewed library staff members to better understand how patrons were currently engaging with their libraries. We also wanted to understand the system that the library staff uses to generate ideas, promote events/media, and collect feedback for future improvements.

Wireframes

Select your preferences for genres. Anonymous data based on your selections will be sent to librarians.

Obtain a fun "personality breakdown" based on your responses.

View recommended events and media based on your preferences. Click on an event to view more info and add to your dashboard.

View upcoming events and media on your dashboard.

Get a notification to leave a review after attending an event / consuming media.

View your friends' activities... maybe you'll like something!

Conclusions

This project challenged me to find an intersection between seemingly traditional institutions and modern social networking. Throughout the project, our mentors pushed us to find the "soul" in our project. Incorporating the personality quiz offered a more tailored and personal experience for users, which I feel elevated it beyond a social calendar app.
